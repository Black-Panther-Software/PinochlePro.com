Frequently Asked Questions

Where did these characters come from?

They were inspired by playing with my wife's grandparents Bob and Shirley. They were so fun, witty, and sarcastic that I decided to memorialize them in this game.

Why do I keep getting similar hands? Why is it not more random?

Pinochle Pro models its shuffling algorithm designed to mimic real-life card shuffling, ensuring fairness and unpredictability. While streaks of similar hands may occur occasionally, it's essential to recognize the extremely low probability of any two hands being exactly alike due to the vast number of possible combinations inherent in a shuffled deck of cards. With 48 cards in a Pinochle deck and over 3 x 1066 possible permutations (that's 48 multiplied by 47, then by 46, and so on down to 1, known as 48 factorial), the chances of repeating a hand are infinitesimally small. For a deeper understanding of the mathematics behind card shuffling, you can explore this article.
